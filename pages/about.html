Me

I, like so many of us, got my start in UX design very early in life. I vividly remember being slowly pushed in a cart around each isle in the fabric store and thinking, "what a terrible experience I am having. How can I make this better for myself?"

On our next trip to the fabric store, I conducted an AB test. I asked to sit in the main cart section. Lined with soft fabrics, where I had room to stretch and move about comfortably for the duration of our errand.

If I can make being in a fabric store a better experienceâ€“ I can definitely fix your internet UX problems.


Joking aside, I do still complain/care a lot about user experience. It is the thing I care most about in any app, website, or even real life. UX is the reason I have Apple products, even though I know Android and Windows are objectively better. It's the reason I hate ads and dont use social media sites*. It's the reason I order groceries for pickup, instead of picking my own produce.

People (users) are more petulant more than ever before, and with a wealth of options in every market the user's path from landing to conversion needs careful handling. A users experience with a product, digital or physical, will determine the success of that product. That is why you want someone like me, solely focused on championing the users perspective.

This fanatical drive to fix UX wherever it is broken, has ruffled feathers in the past. I think logically, I am honest, I can be impatient, I am hard working, and I am sorry if I offend you with a the occasional "ugh that's terrible."

If you've made it this far, congratulations, this is an example of a not-so-great user experience. Close to 300 words on a white page; complete with typos and without heirarchy.
